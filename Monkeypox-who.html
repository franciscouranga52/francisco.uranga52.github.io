"How is the Monkey Pox spread?"
The disease, identified for the first time in monkeys, does not spread easily among people, so experts assure that the risk for the population is low and an expansion like that of the COVID-19 pandemic was not expected.
In order to become infected, close contact with infectious material is necessary. It can occur by touching the skin lesions, like those of chickenpox, which appear on infected persons, but also by "respiratory droplets in prolonged face-to-face contact" or through contaminated objects.

It is not a new disease, it is endemic in some countries of Central and West Africa
According to the World Health Organization, monkeypox virus is endemic in 12 countries: Benin, Cameroon, Central African Republic, Democratic Republic of Congo, Gabon, Ghana (identified only in animals), Ivory Coast, Liberia, Nigeria, Republic of Congo, Sierra Leone and South Sudan.
